## 2. Capitalized Software Development — Detail

### 2.1 Policy & Methodology

The Company capitalizes internal-use software development costs in accordance with **ASC 350-40** (Internal-Use Software) and **ASC 985-20** (Software to be Sold, Leased, or Marketed). Costs are capitalized once a project has progressed beyond the preliminary project stage and management has committed to funding the project with the expectation that it will be completed.

**Capitalization Criteria:**
- Project has been approved by VP Engineering and VP Product
- Technological feasibility has been established
- Resources (engineering FTEs) are allocated to the project
- Preliminary design and planning phase is complete

**Amortization:** Capitalized costs are amortized on a straight-line basis over **3 years** from the date the software is placed into production.

**Capitalization Rate:** Approximately **23–25%** of total R&D spend is capitalized, with the remainder expensed.

**Note for FDD Analysis:** The capitalization rate has been relatively consistent at 23–25%. A lower capitalization rate would increase R&D expense and reduce EBITDA. Sensitivity analysis:

| Capitalization Rate | FY2025E Capitalized | FY2025E R&D Expense | Impact on EBITDA |
|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|-----------------|
| 23.5% (Actual) | $1,800 | $5,847 | — |

| 15.0% | $1,147 | $6,500 | ($653) |

If all R&D were fully expensed (0% capitalization), FY2025E Adjusted EBITDA would decrease from $7,085K to $5,285K. Management believes the current capitalization rate is appropriate and consistent with industry peers.
