## 1. Executive Summary

Test Corp, Inc. ("Test Corp" or the "Company") is a high-growth B2B SaaS company providing workforce management and HR technology solutions to mid-market enterprises across North America. Founded in 2017 and headquartered in Austin, TX, the Company has built a differentiated platform comprising three integrated products — **WorkFlow Pro**, **ShiftGenius**, and **PaySync** — that collectively address the critical workforce planning, scheduling, and payroll needs of organizations with 100 to 5,000 employees.

The Company has demonstrated a compelling financial trajectory, growing revenue from **$18.2 million in FY2023 to an estimated $34.4 million in FY2025**, representing a two-year CAGR of approximately **37%**. Subscription revenue, which constitutes **85% of total revenue**, is underpinned by annual contracts with strong retention dynamics (Net Revenue Retention of **108%**, Logo Churn of **8%**). The Company's gross margin has expanded to **72%** and Adjusted EBITDA is expected to reach **$7.1 million in FY2025** (20.6% margin), reflecting meaningful operating leverage as the platform scales.

Test Corp serves approximately **1,200 mid-market customers** across diverse industries including healthcare, hospitality, retail, manufacturing, and professional services. The customer base is well-diversified, with the top 10 customers representing approximately **18%** of ARR and no single customer exceeding **3.2%** of total revenue.

The Company's shareholders are exploring strategic alternatives, including a potential sale to a growth-oriented partner who can accelerate the Company's expansion through investment in product development, go-to-market capabilities, and strategic M&A. **Apex Growth Partners** has been identified as a preferred buyer given their deep expertise in scaling B2B SaaS platforms.

**Estimated Enterprise Value:** $85M–$110M (8–10x run-rate Adjusted EBITDA)

## 2. Company Overview

### 2.1 History & Mission

Test Corp was founded in 2017 by **Sarah Chen** (CEO) and **Marcus Rivera** (CTO), both former executives at Kronos Incorporated (now UKG). Frustrated by the complexity and cost of enterprise workforce management solutions, they set out to build a modern, cloud-native platform purpose-built for the underserved mid-market segment.

The Company launched its initial product, WorkFlow Pro, in Q3 2018 after 18 months of development. Early traction came through direct sales to regional healthcare systems and hospitality groups in Texas. By 2019, the Company had secured $4.5 million in Series A funding from Lone Star Ventures and expanded its sales team beyond the Austin market.

### 2.3 Products

**WorkFlow Pro** (Core Platform — 65% of subscription revenue)
The flagship workforce management platform providing time & attendance tracking, employee self-service portals, compliance management, PTO tracking, and workforce analytics. Deployed as a multi-tenant SaaS application with configurable workflows and role-based access controls.

**ShiftGenius** (Scheduling Module — 25% of subscription revenue)
An AI-powered shift scheduling engine that optimizes employee schedules based on demand forecasting, labor regulations, employee preferences, and skills matching. Launched in 2020, ShiftGenius has become a key differentiator and upsell driver, with 62% attach rate among WorkFlow Pro customers.

**PaySync** (Payroll Integration — 10% of subscription revenue)
A bidirectional payroll integration hub connecting Test Corp's platform with major payroll providers (ADP, Paychex, Gusto, Paylocity, and QuickBooks Payroll). PaySync eliminates manual data entry, reduces payroll errors, and supports multi-state tax compliance. Launched in 2022 with a 45% attach rate and growing.

## 4. Product & Technology

### 4.1 Technical Architecture

Test Corp's platform is built on a modern, cloud-native architecture:

- **Infrastructure:** AWS (primary), with multi-region deployment (us-east-1, us-west-2, ca-central-1)
- **Backend:** Microservices architecture using Node.js and Python; event-driven with Apache Kafka
- **Frontend:** React.js SPA with responsive design; native iOS and Android mobile apps (React Native)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (primary), Redis (caching), Elasticsearch (search & analytics)
- **AI/ML:** Python-based ML pipeline using scikit-learn and TensorFlow for demand forecasting and schedule optimization
- **DevOps:** Kubernetes (EKS), Terraform for IaC, GitHub Actions CI/CD, Datadog monitoring
- **Security:** SOC 2 Type II certified, AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit, annual penetration testing

## 5. Go-to-Market

### 5.1 Sales Motion

Test Corp employs a **hybrid sales model**:

- **Inside Sales (60% of new bookings):** SDR → AE model targeting inbound leads from content marketing, webinars, and partner referrals. Average deal size: $22,000 ARR. Sales cycle: 45 days.
- **Mid-Market Field Sales (30% of new bookings):** Regional AEs targeting accounts with 1,000+ employees via outbound prospecting and industry events. Average deal size: $65,000 ARR. Sales cycle: 90 days.
- **Channel Partners (10% of new bookings):** Referral partnerships with HR consultancies, payroll providers, and HRIS platforms. 48 active partners.

## 8. Growth Strategy

Test Corp's management team has identified five strategic growth vectors:

1. **Product Expansion:** Launch "TalentFlow" recruiting module in H2 2026, addressing a $1.2B adjacent market. Early customer interviews indicate strong demand and a potential $3–$4 PEPM pricing opportunity.

2. **Geographic Expansion:** Extend Canadian operations to a full go-to-market presence; explore UK market entry in 2027 leveraging the existing Toronto engineering team.

3. **Upmarket Push:** Develop enterprise-grade features (multi-entity management, advanced analytics, custom APIs) to address organizations with 5,000–15,000 employees without increasing sales complexity.

4. **Platform & Integrations:** Expand the integration ecosystem to 50+ connectors (currently 28) including deeper ERP, HCM, and benefits administration integrations. Launch an Integration Marketplace in 2026.

5. **Strategic M&A:** Pursue tuck-in acquisitions of complementary point solutions in adjacent categories (e.g., employee engagement, learning management, benefits administration) to expand the platform's value proposition.

## 11. Risk Factors

- **Customer Concentration (Low Risk):** Top 10 customers represent ~19% of ARR, though MedFirst Health Systems at 3.2% warrants monitoring.
- **Key Person Risk (Moderate):** The Company is founder-led. Sarah Chen and Marcus Rivera are deeply involved in product strategy and customer relationships. Mitigated by the strong management bench.
- **Competitive Dynamics (Moderate):** UKG and ADP have significantly greater resources. Paylocity is increasingly encroaching on Test Corp's mid-market positioning.
- **Canadian Operations (Low):** Toronto office introduces FX exposure (~8% of costs in CAD) and Canadian employment law complexity.
- **Technology Risk (Low):** Platform has experienced two notable outages in the past 24 months (99.92% uptime vs. 99.95% SLA target). Engineering team has invested heavily in reliability improvements.
- **Economic Sensitivity (Moderate):** Mid-market customers may delay purchasing decisions or reduce headcount during economic downturns, directly impacting PEPM-based revenue.
- **Sales Capacity (Moderate):** Growth plan requires continued investment in sales hiring. Quota attainment has averaged 82% across the team, with ramp time of 6–9 months for new AEs.
