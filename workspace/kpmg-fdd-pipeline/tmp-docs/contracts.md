## Master Subscription Agreement — Standard Terms

All customer contracts are executed under Test Corp's Master Subscription Agreement (MSA), with order forms specifying product, pricing, and term. Key standard terms include:

| Provision | Standard Term |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Agreement Structure** | MSA + Order Form(s) |
| **Billing** | Annual in advance (net 30 days) |
| **Auto-Renewal** | Yes — automatic 1-year renewal unless 60-day written notice |
| **Price Escalator** | 3–5% annual increase at renewal (standard clause in most contracts) |
| **Termination for Cause** | 30-day cure period for material breach |
| **Termination for Convenience** | Not permitted during term; customer remains liable for full contract value |
| **SLA — Uptime** | 99.9% monthly uptime guarantee |
| **SLA — Credit** | 5% service credit for each 0.1% below 99.9% (capped at 30% of monthly fee) |
| **SLA — Support Response** | Critical: 1 hour / High: 4 hours / Medium: 8 hours / Low: 24 hours |
| **Data Ownership** | Customer retains ownership of all customer data |
| **Data Portability** | Customer entitled to data export upon termination (CSV/API) |
| **Indemnification** | Mutual indemnification for IP infringement and data breach |
| **Liability Cap** | 12 months of fees paid (mutual) |
| **Insurance Requirements** | Test Corp maintains $5M cyber liability, $2M E&O, $1M CGL |
| **Governing Law** | State of Delaware |
| **Dispute Resolution** | Binding arbitration (AAA, Austin, TX) |

## Key Legal Observations

1. **Favorable Termination Provisions:** No customer has termination-for-convenience rights. All contracts require payment through the full term, which is a strong downside protection.

2. **Auto-Renewal Risk:** While 96% of contracts auto-renew, the 60–90 day notice windows mean that churn decisions are typically finalized well in advance.

3. **MFN Clauses:** 8 contracts (including RetailMax, the #3 customer) contain Most Favored Nation pricing clauses. This could constrain upmarket pricing if the Company offers lower per-unit rates to large enterprise customers in the future.

4. **Liability Exposure:** Standard liability cap of 12 months' fees. Two large healthcare customers (MedFirst, Sunrise) negotiated enhanced caps of 24 months' fees due to HIPAA-related data breach indemnification.

5. **Change of Control:** No contracts contain change-of-control termination provisions. The proposed acquisition by Apex Growth Partners would not trigger any termination rights.

6. **Pending Disputes:** No active contract disputes or litigation related to customer agreements as of January 2026.
