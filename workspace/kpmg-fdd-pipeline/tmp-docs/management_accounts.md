## Commentary — Monthly Trends

### Revenue Seasonality
- **Q4 strength:** December revenue of $3,212K reflects the annual Q4 sales push, with significant new bookings closing in November and December (typical for B2B SaaS selling to mid-market HR buyers planning for new calendar year implementations).
- **Q1 implementation surge:** March implementation revenue ($298K) reflects onboarding of Q4 bookings.
- **Summer softness:** July-August show slightly lower implementation revenue as customer decision-makers take vacation.

### Gross Margin Dynamics
- Gross margin ranges from 71.3% (January) to 73.9% (December), reflecting:
  - **Hosting cost efficiency:** AWS Reserved Instance savings phased in during Q2 2025, reducing per-unit hosting costs.
  - **Revenue mix:** Higher subscription mix in December (no seasonal implementation dip) improves margin.
  - **Support staffing:** Two support hires in Q1 temporarily compressed margin.

### Operating Expense Trends
- **S&M:** March spike ($772K) driven by attendance at HR Technology Conference ($85K event spend) and Q1 accelerator commissions ($30K above average).
- **R&D:** Steady increase reflects 8 engineering hires throughout the year (net of 2 departures). December dip reflects holiday PTO accrual timing.
- **G&A:** Relatively stable at ~$300K/month. Q1 and Q4 spikes in professional services relate to annual audit preparation and transaction-related advisory costs.

### EBITDA Trajectory
- EBITDA expanded from $447K (17.5% margin) in January to $881K (27.4% margin) in December, demonstrating strong operating leverage.
- H2 2025 EBITDA margin averaged 22.3%, compared to 18.4% in H1 2025.
- **Caution:** December EBITDA of $881K is elevated due to: (1) Q4 revenue seasonality, (2) lower relative marketing spend, and (3) timing of bonus accruals. Normalized run-rate EBITDA is closer to $650–700K/month.
