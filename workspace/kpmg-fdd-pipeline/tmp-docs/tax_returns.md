## 2. §174 R&D Capitalization Analysis

### 2.1 Background

Under the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA), beginning in tax year 2022, IRC §174 requires companies to capitalize and amortize research and experimental (R&E) expenditures rather than deducting them currently. Domestic R&E is amortized over **5 years** and foreign R&E (i.e., R&D conducted in Canada) over **15 years**.

This has created a significant **book-tax timing difference** for Test Corp, as the Company continues to expense R&D for book purposes while capitalizing for tax purposes.

## 10. Transaction Tax Considerations (for Apex Growth Partners)

The following tax matters are relevant to the proposed transaction:

1. **Asset vs. Stock Purchase:** The Company is a C-corporation. A stock purchase would result in no step-up in tax basis. An asset purchase (or §338(h)(10) election) would provide Apex with a step-up in the tax basis of assets, generating future tax deductions. Management has been informed that Apex prefers a stock acquisition structure.

2. **§174 Deferred Tax Liability:** The accumulated §174 DTL of ~$2,692K will carry over in a stock acquisition. The buyer should model the reversal schedule (amortization deductions in future years) against forecasted taxable income.

3. **NOL Carryforward:** The remaining $597K NOL is expected to be fully utilized before closing. If not, a §382 limitation will apply based on the equity value of the Company multiplied by the long-term tax-exempt rate.

4. **R&D Credits:** The Company's R&D credit methodology and documentation have been reviewed by Baker Tilly and are expected to withstand IRS scrutiny. Credits are ongoing and represent ~$260K annual benefit.

5. **QSBS (§1202):** Certain shareholders may qualify for the Qualified Small Business Stock exclusion under §1202, potentially excluding up to $10M (or 10x basis) of gain from federal taxation. Individual shareholder eligibility should be confirmed by their personal tax advisors.

6. **Deferred Revenue:** Under Rev. Proc. 2004-34, the buyer may inherit the obligation to recognize deferred revenue (~$8.6M) without receiving a corresponding tax deduction in an asset purchase scenario. In a stock purchase, this is a non-issue as the existing entity continues.
