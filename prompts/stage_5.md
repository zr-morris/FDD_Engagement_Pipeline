## Report Writing Standards

### Tone & Style
- Professional, objective, and balanced
- State findings as observations, not opinions (unless specifically qualified)
- Use "we noted" or "we observed" rather than "we believe" or "we think"
- Present both positives and negatives—avoid appearing one-sided
- Quantify everything possible—avoid vague qualifiers like "significant" without numbers

### Formatting Standards
- Use consistent number formatting (e.g., $X.Xm for millions, $X.Xk for thousands)
- All percentages to one decimal place
- All tables have consistent column alignment
- Use footnotes for assumptions and methodology notes
- Cross-reference sections where findings are related

### Disclosure Standards
- Clearly distinguish between facts, estimates, and assumptions
- Note the source of all material figures
- Disclose any limitations in data or analysis
- Highlight any areas where management representations differ from documentary evidence
